Some Notes:
I'm not the most experienced at working out, but I'm happy to share what I do in hopes that it might work for you. I do change the actual excercises that I do frequently, but the targeted muscles and the actual Push, Pull, Leg split does not change. I like this split because it allows me to hit every muscle and have 72-hours of rest in between every workout. This makes it much easier to actually workout six days a week when I do workout six days a week.


Every workout I do is preceded by at least 10 - 15 minutes of cardio. Normally this is jogging on a treadmill, but it could be on a rowing machine, bike, stairmaster or anything else. I find this and some stretches to be a good enough warm-up.


With every workouut I do, I aim to do 10 reps in every set. When I can do 10 reps easily, that's when I know to add more weight. But I always workout to failure. This isn't always ideal, research seems to suggest that getting withing 1 - 2 reps of failure is more ideal. However, I'm still fairly new to working out, and am not confident in my ability to estimate this.
