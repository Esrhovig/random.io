About

nexus1:makc.co$ about.html

I first began work on this site back in 2010. Back then, YouTube and other video platforms were around, and widely used; but it was also still quite common to find videos hosted on independent sites. So, originally my site was just this, a home for all of my original content; all of which has since been relocated to my YouTube channel.

Of course, as with any simple endeavor, things quickly grew; and my sight became a home for not only videos that I produced, but also After Effects Presets, and other downloadable content. Next, the sight grew into a portfolio for my motion graphics clients. Now the site is what it is today, a hodgepodge of unorganized none-sense masquerading as a product made by someone who knows what they're doing.

Surprisingly enough, however, through all the iterations my site has gone through the years, I've never created a completely custom site, on my own. I relied on tools like Adobe Muse, Squarespace, & Wordpress for the better part of a decade; until one day, I created a new folder on my desktop and started building what would eventually become this:

MK-XVI, nexus1, mack.co-2019, v16; there are many names I can and have called this version of my site; but for now, you can think of what you're looking at as, one of my most successful experiments and learning experiences.

I began work on this site about mid-way through 2019 with no legitimate front-end, or back-end web development experiment. The goal was to force myself to learn a skill that I had flirted with since Junior High. While I may be relatively new to web development, I do think there are a few interesting things about my site.

First, this site is built without the use of and frameworks or libraries whatsoever; something that is of course increasingly rare in 2019. I don't have anything against using React or Less or jQuery for building a web site, and more than once while building my site I ran into a problem that could have been easily solved by one of these systems. But at least for the first swing, I've built this entire website using good old-fashioned HTML, CSS, & JavaScript. (Plus a little bit of PHP to make some forms work)

As the site is a bit of an oddball, and since I'm new to this, I appreciate anyone who wants's to criticize my code. So, my entire site's code is available in a GitHub repository. Additionally, there are always two public versions of my site: The current live version of my site, which is available at makc.co; and a beta version, which is tied to my site's repository and will include any new features that may be in the pipeline. This version of my site is always available at makccr.github.io
